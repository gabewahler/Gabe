Austrailas Nature and wildlife

One of Australia's most well known landmarks is not on land at all the “Great barrier reef” the largest saltwater reef in the world is 134,634 mi². It is home to over 1500 species of fish and 411 different types of coral, adding to nearly 2000 different sea wildlife including sea turtles starfish and many other animals. The ocean life isn't the only thing Australia has to offer. With different biomes such as temperate forest desert rainforest and savanna there are all sorts of animals. To name a few there are 140 different species of snakes 2,400 different species of spiders 860 different species of reptiles 800 species of mammals and 829 different birds. This equals roughly 8,111 different species that call Australia their home.

Australian Cities

Sydney

Sydney is one of the most popular cities in the world and with a population of 4.6 million.
It is also home to the sydney opera house one of the most famous landmarks in the world and the most
famous from Australia by far. Another nation landmark in sydney is the “Sydney harbor bridge”
that is typically called the “coathanger” for its arched shape.

Melbourne

Melbourn is a very interesting and diverse city, with a population of nearly 5 billion people it is also one of the largest in Australia.
But not only people melbourn is the official fox capital of the world . With between 6 and 23 foxes per square kilometer,
although it is still rare to see one. Melbourn also was known for having public bathing in the yarra river
now there are private bathing boxes. A very popular landmark in Melbourne is the
flinders street the first rail station in australia.

Brisbane

Brisbane is the capital of queensland the “sunshine state”. It has a population of 1.6 and is the third largest in australia.
The city sits on the Brisbane river Brisbane is known for their open lifestyle and very cheap transport.
A lot of the restaurants will have very big windows or even open walls combining the inside with the outside. Also the transportation is extremely
cheap you could get a train ticket to anywhere in Brisbane for below $5.

Perth

Perth also known as the “city of light” is home to nearly 2 million people. It is placed right on the indian ocean. It is known for having
wonderful weather and being home to many wealthy people. With its booming mining market
it is home to the most self made millionaires in the world. It is also home to the oldest operating
mint in the world “perth mint” which has been operating since 1899.
